# PM Career Development Blueprint

## PM Levels Overview

| Level | Title | Scope | Impact |
|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| 1 | Associate PM | Features | Team |
| 2 | Product Manager | Feature set | Cross-team |
| 3 | Senior PM | Product area | Business unit |
| 4 | Principal/Staff PM | Product line | Company |
| 5 | Director PM | Portfolio | Strategic |

## Competency Framework

### Core Competencies

| Competency | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Product Sense** | Understanding what makes a good product |
| **Execution** | Shipping quality products on time |
| **Strategic Thinking** | Connecting work to business outcomes |
| **Leadership** | Influencing and developing others |
| **Technical** | Understanding technology constraints and possibilities |
| **Communication** | Effective written and verbal communication |

| Competency | L1 | L2 | L3 | L4 | L5 |
|------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Product Sense | Learning | Applying | Mastering | Teaching | Innovating |

| Strategic | Understanding | Contributing | Driving | Shaping | Defining |
| Leadership | Self | Project | Team | Organization | Industry |

## Level Expectations

### Level 1: Associate PM

**Scope**: Features within a product
**Impact**: Team productivity

**Key Behaviors**:
- Writes clear requirements
- Coordinates with engineering and design
- Tracks progress and blockers
- Communicates status effectively

**Growth Focus**:
- Learn the craft fundamentals
- Build technical understanding
- Develop stakeholder skills
- Practice data analysis

### Level 2: Product Manager

**Scope**: Feature set or small product
**Impact**: Cross-team coordination

**Key Behaviors**:
- Owns feature end-to-end
- Makes prioritization decisions
- Drives cross-functional alignment
- Measures and improves outcomes

**Growth Focus**:
- Develop product intuition
- Build strategic thinking
- Expand influence skills
- Master execution

### Level 3: Senior PM

**Scope**: Product area or major initiative
**Impact**: Business unit results

**Key Behaviors**:
- Sets product area strategy
- Mentors junior PMs
- Drives significant business outcomes
- Influences beyond product org

**Growth Focus**:
- Develop strategic vision
- Build organizational influence
- Master ambiguity
- Develop others

### Level 4: Principal/Staff PM

**Scope**: Product line or critical initiative
**Impact**: Company-level outcomes

**Key Behaviors**:
- Defines product strategy
- Shapes organizational approach
- Solves hardest problems
- Represents product externally

**Growth Focus**:
- Executive influence
- Strategic partnership
- Thought leadership
- Organizational design

### Level 5: Director PM

**Scope**: Portfolio of products
**Impact**: Strategic direction

**Key Behaviors**:
- Sets portfolio strategy
- Builds and leads PM team
- Drives organizational excellence
- Partners with executives

**Growth Focus**:
- Executive leadership
- Organizational transformation
- Industry influence
- Talent development

## Development Actions

### For Growth at Any Level

1. **Seek feedback** - Regular 1:1s, 360 reviews
2. **Take on stretch assignments** - Beyond comfort zone
3. **Find mentors** - Learn from experienced PMs
4. **Learn continuously** - Books, courses, communities
5. **Reflect regularly** - What worked, what didn't

### Level-Specific Development

| Moving To | Key Development Actions |
|-----------|------------------------|
| L2 | Own a feature end-to-end, practice prioritization |
| L3 | Lead a significant initiative, mentor a junior PM |
| L4 | Drive cross-team strategy, influence executives |
| L5 | Build and lead team, shape organizational practices |
