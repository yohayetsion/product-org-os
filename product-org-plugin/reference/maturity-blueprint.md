# Product Organization Maturity Blueprint

## Maturity Levels Overview

| Level | Name | Focus | Decision Style |
|-------|------|-------|----------------|
| 1 | **Enabling** | Survival, finding product-market fit | Founder-driven |
| 2 | **Established** | Scaling, building processes | Team-driven |
| 3 | **Company Leading** | Optimizing, cross-functional excellence | System-driven |
| 4 | **Market Leading** | Innovating, setting industry standards | Culture-driven |

## V2V Operating System Adoption by Maturity

| Level | V2V Phase Adoption | Skills Emphasis | Principle Validators |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **L1: Enabling** | Focus on Phases 4-5 (execution, outcomes) | `/prd`, `/feature-spec`, `/launch-readiness` | Minimal - `/phase-check` |
| **L2: Established** | Add Phases 2-3 (decisions, commitments) | `/decision-record`, `/product-roadmap`, `/gtm-brief` | `/ownership-map`, `/customer-value-trace` |
| **L3: Company Leading** | Full V2V flow including Phase 1 & 6 | `/strategic-bet`, `/commitment-check`, `/outcome-review` | All 5 validators regularly |
| **L4: Market Leading** | Advanced V2V with portfolio orchestration | `/portfolio-tradeoff`, `/decision-quality-audit`, `/principles scorecard` | Systematic audits |

## Assessment Skills

- `/maturity-check` - Assess organizational maturity for a dimension
- `/pm-level-check` - Assess PM competency level
- `/decision-quality-audit` - Audit recent decisions for quality

## Level 1: Enabling

**Characteristics**:
- Basic product capabilities exist
- Reactive to market and customers
- Founder/leader makes most decisions
- Informal processes

**Focus Areas**:
- Find product-market fit
- Learn rapidly from customers
- Build minimal viable processes
- Hire foundational team

**Success Indicators**:
- Product exists and has customers
- Basic metrics tracked
- Team can ship features

## Level 2: Established

**Characteristics**:
- Consistent processes in place
- Proactive planning
- Team makes decisions within boundaries
- Documented ways of working

**Focus Areas**:
- Scale what works
- Implement repeatable processes
- Develop team capabilities
- Cross-functional coordination

**Success Indicators**:
- Predictable delivery
- Documented decisions
- Team operates independently
- Customer satisfaction tracked

## Level 3: Company Leading

**Characteristics**:
- Best practices implemented
- Strategic planning integrated
- Cross-functional excellence
- Systems and tooling mature

**Focus Areas**:
- Optimize end-to-end
- Develop thought leadership
- Build organizational capabilities
- Drive strategic impact

**Success Indicators**:
- Industry-competitive practices
- Strategic influence in company
- High team performance
- Measurable business impact

## Level 4: Market Leading

**Characteristics**:
- Industry leadership
- Continuous innovation
- Culture of excellence
- External recognition

**Focus Areas**:
- Set industry standards
- Innovate on practices
- Develop next generation leaders
- Shape market dynamics

**Success Indicators**:
- External recognition
- Talent magnet
- Copied by competitors
- Market influence

## Maturity by Dimension

| Dimension | L1: Enabling | L2: Established | L3: Company Leading | L4: Market Leading |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Strategy** | Reactive | Quarterly plans | Annual strategy | Multi-year vision |
| **Decisions** | Informal | Basic records | Full charters | Systematic audits |
| **Roadmapping** | Feature lists | Theme-based | Strategic bets | Portfolio strategy |
| **GTM** | Ad-hoc | Playbooks | Strategic choice | Market shaping |
| **Requirements** | Verbal | Basic specs | Full process | Co-creation |
| **Outcomes** | Anecdotal | Basic tracking | Systematic review | Predictive |

## Progression Guidance

### Moving from Level 1 to Level 2
- Document key processes
- Implement basic templates
- Hire/develop team leads
- Establish regular cadences

### Moving from Level 2 to Level 3
- Optimize cross-functional flows
- Implement advanced tooling
- Develop strategic planning
- Build measurement systems

### Moving from Level 3 to Level 4
- Innovate on practices
- Build external presence
- Develop thought leadership
- Create learning organization
