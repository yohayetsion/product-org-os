# Maturity-Appropriate Guidance

This rule provides maturity-level context for all product org work.

## Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Characteristics |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Enabling** | Basic capabilities, reactive, founder-driven |
| 2 | **Established** | Consistent processes, proactive, team-driven |
| 3 | **Company Leading** | Best practices, strategic, cross-functional excellence |
| 4 | **Market Leading** | Industry leadership, innovation, setting standards |

## Adapting to Maturity Level

### Level 1: Enabling
- Keep processes lightweight
- Focus on quick wins
- Document just enough
- Prioritize learning over perfection

### Level 2: Established
- Implement consistent templates
- Build repeatable processes
- Develop team capabilities
- Balance speed with quality

### Level 3: Company Leading
- Optimize processes
- Share best practices
- Invest in tooling
- Focus on cross-functional excellence

### Level 4: Market Leading
- Innovate on processes
- Influence industry
- Develop thought leadership
- Continuous improvement culture

## Process Scaling

| Process | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Decision Records | Informal notes | Basic template | Full charter | Systematic audits |
| Roadmapping | Founder vision | Quarterly themes | Annual strategy | Multi-year planning |
| GTM | Reactive | Playbooks | Strategic choice | Market shaping |
| Requirements | Verbal | Basic PRD | Full spec process | Customer co-creation |

## V2V Operating Principle

> "Scalable systems are processes that work as the organization grows."

Match process complexity to organizational maturity. Don't over-engineer for a startup. Don't under-invest for scale.
