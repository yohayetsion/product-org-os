## Partnership Type Taxonomy

### By Structure

| Type | Commitment | Duration | Governance | Example |
|------|-----------|----------|------------|---------|
| **Referral** | Low | Ongoing | Informal | Partner refers customers for fee |
| **Reseller** | Medium | 1-3 years | Contract | Partner resells your product |
| **Technology Integration** | Medium | Ongoing | API agreement | Product-to-product integration |
| **Co-Development** | High | Project-based | Joint steering | Build something together |
| **Joint Venture** | Very High | Multi-year | Board + legal entity | Shared entity for new market |
| **Strategic Alliance** | High | Multi-year | Executive sponsors | Deep multi-dimensional partnership |
| **Licensing** | Medium | Term-based | License agreement | IP/technology licensing |
| **White Label / OEM** | High | Multi-year | SLA + contract | Partner brands your product |

### By Strategic Intent

| Intent | Partnership Type | Success Metric |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Market access** | Reseller, referral, channel | Revenue through partner |
| **Capability extension** | Technology integration, co-development | Product capability gap closed |
| **Risk sharing** | JV, strategic alliance | Investment leverage ratio |
| **Speed to market** | OEM, licensing, integration | Time saved vs. build |
| **Ecosystem building** | API/integration, marketplace | Ecosystem participants, stickiness |
| **Geographic expansion** | Reseller, JV, alliance | New market revenue |

## Partnership Evaluation Framework

### Strategic Value Assessment

| Dimension | Weight | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Strategic alignment | 25% | | Mission/vision compatibility |
| Market access value | 20% | | Customers/segments reached |
| Capability complement | 20% | | What they bring that we lack |
| Financial potential | 15% | | Revenue/cost impact |
| Competitive moat | 10% | | Does this strengthen our position? |
| Execution capability | 10% | | Can they deliver on commitments? |

**Threshold**: Score >= 3.5 for active pursuit, >= 4.0 for executive sponsorship.

### Partner Due Diligence

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|---------------|
| **Financial health** | Can they invest in the partnership? Will they exist in 3 years? |
| **Cultural fit** | Decision-making speed, risk tolerance, innovation appetite |
| **Organizational capacity** | Do they have people to staff the partnership? |
| **Strategic intent** | Why do they want this? Is it core or peripheral for them? |
| **Track record** | How have their other partnerships performed? |
| **Competitive conflicts** | Do they partner with our competitors? |

## Partnership Structuring

### Value Exchange Models

| Model | Description | Risk Distribution |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Revenue share** | Split revenue from joint customers | Shared proportionally |
| **Referral fee** | Fixed fee or % per qualified referral | Low risk, low reward |
| **Cost sharing** | Split development or go-to-market costs | Shared equally or proportionally |
| **Equity exchange** | Minority stake investments | Aligned long-term incentives |
| **Performance-based** | Payment tied to outcomes | Risk on performer |
| **Contribution-based** | Value proportional to input | Fair but hard to measure |

### Agreement Structure

```
Partnership Agreement Anatomy:

1. Purpose & Scope
   - Strategic objectives
   - Activities in scope / out of scope
   - Exclusivity terms (if any)

2. Governance
   - Executive sponsors (named individuals)
   - Joint steering committee (composition, cadence)
   - Decision-making authority matrix
   - Escalation path

3. Commercial Terms
   - Revenue/cost sharing model
   - Payment terms and mechanics
   - Investment commitments (if any)
   - Volume commitments (if any)

4. Operating Model
   - Joint go-to-market activities
   - Integration/technical requirements
   - Support responsibilities (L1/L2/L3)
   - Training and enablement

5. IP & Data
   - Pre-existing IP ownership
   - Joint IP ownership/licensing
   - Data sharing and usage rights
   - Confidentiality obligations

6. Performance & Review
   - KPIs and success metrics
   - Review cadence (quarterly/annual)
   - Adjustment mechanisms
   - Underperformance remedies

7. Term & Termination
   - Initial term and renewal
   - Termination for convenience (notice period)
   - Termination for cause (triggers)
   - Wind-down obligations
   - Post-termination IP/data handling
```

## Partnership Lifecycle Management

### Phase 1: Discovery & Evaluation (4-8 weeks)

| Activity | Output |
|----------|--------|
| Market mapping | Partnership landscape map |
| Strategic fit assessment | Scorecard per potential partner |
| Executive alignment | Internal sponsorship secured |
| Preliminary discussions | Mutual interest confirmed |

### Phase 2: Negotiation & Structuring (6-12 weeks)

| Activity | Output |
|----------|--------|
| Term sheet / MOU | Agreed principles |
| Legal agreement drafting | Partnership agreement |
| Integration planning | Technical integration plan |
| GTM planning | Joint go-to-market plan |
| Executive sign-off | Signed agreement |

### Phase 3: Launch & Ramp (3-6 months)

| Activity | Output |
|----------|--------|
| Internal enablement | Sales, support, marketing trained |
| External announcement | Press release, blog, social |
| Integration go-live | Technical integration working |
| First joint customers | Pipeline generation started |
| 90-day review | Performance vs. plan assessment |

### Phase 4: Optimize & Scale (Ongoing)

| Activity | Cadence |
|----------|---------|
| Steering committee | Quarterly |
| Performance review | Monthly metrics, quarterly deep-dive |
| Joint business planning | Annual |
| Partnership health check | Semi-annual |
| Executive sponsors meeting | Semi-annual |

### Phase 5: Evolve or Exit

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Strong performance | Expand scope, deepen investment |
| Moderate performance | Identify optimization opportunities |
| Underperformance | Corrective action plan with timeline |
| Strategic misalignment | Renegotiate terms or plan wind-down |
| Partner acquisition by competitor | Assess continuity, prepare alternatives |

## Channel Partnership Specifics

### Channel Program Tiers

| Tier | Commitment | Benefits | Typical Structure |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Registered** | Minimal | Deal registration, basic materials | Open enrollment |
| **Silver** | Revenue target | Training, co-marketing funds | Certification required |
| **Gold** | Higher revenue + certs | Priority leads, dedicated support | Annual review |
| **Platinum** | Strategic | Joint planning, executive access | Invitation only |

### Channel Conflict Management

| Scenario | Resolution |
|----------|-----------|
| Partner vs. direct sales | Clear rules of engagement, deal registration |
| Partner vs. partner | Territory or vertical segmentation |
| Multiple partners on same deal | First-to-register or split |
| Partner brings deal, wants to go direct | Honor deal registration period |

## Partnership Health Metrics

### Operational Metrics

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Joint pipeline generated | Growing QoQ | Flat | Declining |
| Deal conversion rate | >= Industry avg | Below average | Significantly below |
| Time to first deal | < 90 days | 90-180 days | > 180 days |
| Integration uptime | >= 99.5% | 99-99.5% | < 99% |
| Support escalation rate | < 10% | 10-25% | > 25% |

### Relationship Metrics

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Executive engagement | Active, regular | Sporadic | Absent |
| Joint planning participation | Full attendance | Partial | Minimal |
| Responsiveness (avg reply time) | < 24 hrs | 24-72 hrs | > 72 hrs |
| Mutual investment | Both investing | One-sided | Neither investing |
| Net Promoter (internal survey) | >= 8 | 6-7 | < 6 |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Mitigation |
|-------------|------|-----------|
| **Partnership theater** | Announcement without execution | Require operational plan before PR |
| **Asymmetric commitment** | One side does all the work | Balanced investment requirements |
| **Champion dependency** | Partnership dies when champion leaves | Multi-threaded relationships |
| **Integration only** | No commercial model | Require GTM plan alongside integration |
| **Competitor hedging** | Partner works with everyone | Tiered exclusivity or differentiation |
| **Scope creep** | Partnership expands without governance | Formal scope change process |

## Joint Venture Specifics

### JV Decision Criteria

Use JV when:
- Significant capital investment required
- Regulatory requirements demand local entity
- Neither party wants to fully commit resources internally
- Risk is too high for one party alone
- Local market knowledge + global capability needed

### JV Governance Structure

```
Board of Directors (50/50 or majority/minority)
  ├── Strategic decisions (both parties)
  ├── Operating committee (day-to-day)
  ├── Finance committee (budgets, capital)
  └── Management team (dedicated JV leadership)
```

### JV Exit Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Put/Call options** | One party can force buy/sell at formula price |
| **Drag-along** | Majority can force minority to sell |
| **Tag-along** | Minority can join majority sale |
| **Shotgun clause** | One party names price, other buys or sells at that price |
| **IPO** | Take JV public |
| **Dissolution** | Wind down and distribute assets |

## Common Pitfalls

- Partnership economics must be modeled with real numbers — placeholder frameworks are fine, fabricated projections are not
- Exclusivity clauses have long-term implications — always flag for legal review
- Channel partnerships require enablement investment — don't assume partners self-serve
