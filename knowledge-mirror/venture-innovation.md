## CVC Program Design

### Strategic vs. Financial Orientation

| Dimension | Strategic CVC | Financial CVC | Hybrid |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|--------|
| **Primary goal** | Strategic value | Financial returns | Both weighted |
| **Investment thesis** | Aligned to corporate strategy | Best returns available | Strategy-first, returns required |
| **Deal flow** | Industry/sector focused | Broad | Sector + adjacent |
| **Board involvement** | Active, strategic guidance | Passive/observer | Selective involvement |
| **Time horizon** | 5-10 years | 3-7 years | 5-7 years |
| **Success metric** | Strategic initiatives launched | IRR/MOIC | Blended scorecard |

### Program Structure Options

| Structure | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|------|------|
| **On-balance-sheet** | Simple, flexible | P&L volatility, corporate approval speed |
| **Dedicated fund** | LP structure, clear mandate | Setup cost, governance overhead |
| **Fund-of-funds** | Diversification, access to top VCs | Indirect, less strategic control |
| **Accelerator/incubator** | Early access, culture infusion | Resource intensive, low hit rate |
| **Scout program** | Broad network, low cost | Quality control, attribution |

### Investment Parameters

| Parameter | Early Stage (Seed-A) | Growth Stage (B-C) | Late Stage (D+) |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Check size | $250K - $2M | $2M - $10M | $5M - $25M |
| Ownership target | 5-15% | 3-10% | 1-5% |
| Board seat | Observer | Board or observer | Observer |
| Follow-on reserved | 2-3x initial | 1-2x initial | Selective |
| Time to return | 7-10 years | 5-7 years | 3-5 years |

## Investment Evaluation Framework

### Screening Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Strategic relevance | 30% | | Alignment with corporate priorities |
| Market opportunity | 20% | | TAM/SAM size and growth |
| Team quality | 20% | | Founders, experience, grit |
| Product/technology | 15% | | Differentiation, defensibility |
| Financial profile | 15% | | Unit economics, capital efficiency |

**Threshold**: Score >= 3.5 for investment committee presentation.

### Investment Memo Structure

```
1. Executive Summary
   - Company, stage, ask, strategic rationale

2. Company Overview
   - Founded, HQ, team size, funding history
   - Product/service description
   - Business model

3. Strategic Thesis
   - Why this matters to our corporate strategy
   - Specific strategic value expected
   - Strategic initiatives this could enable

4. Market Analysis
   - TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology
   - Market dynamics and trends
   - Competitive landscape

5. Team Assessment
   - Founders background and track record
   - Key hires and organizational gaps
   - Culture and values alignment

6. Product & Technology
   - Product maturity and roadmap
   - Technology differentiation
   - IP portfolio and protection

7. Financial Analysis
   - Historical financials (if available)
   - Key metrics (MRR, growth rate, burn, runway)
   - Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback)
   - Scenario analysis (bull/base/bear)

8. Deal Terms
   - Valuation and basis
   - Round structure
   - Key terms (liquidation prefs, anti-dilution, etc.)
   - Co-investors and lead

9. Risks & Mitigations
   - Top 5 risks with mitigation strategies
   - What would make this investment fail?

10. Recommendation
    - Invest / Pass / Conditional
    - If invest: amount, structure, conditions
```

## Portfolio Management

### Portfolio Construction

| Principle | Guideline |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Diversification** | 15-25 companies across 3-5 thesis areas |
| **Stage mix** | 50% early, 30% growth, 20% late |
| **Geography** | Aligned with corporate footprint |
| **Vintage years** | Deploy over 3-4 years, not all at once |
| **Reserve ratio** | 40-50% of fund for follow-on |
| **Concentration limit** | No single investment > 15% of fund |

### Portfolio Monitoring

| Frequency | Activity | Participants |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Monthly | Portfolio company updates review | Investment team |
| Quarterly | Portfolio review with strategic assessment | CVC + business unit leaders |
| Semi-annual | Deep dive on each company | Investment team + company CEO |
| Annual | Portfolio strategy review | CVC + executive team |

### Key Metrics by Stage

| Metric | Pre-Revenue | Early Revenue | Growth |
|--------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Product milestones | Primary | Secondary | - |
| User/customer count | If applicable | Primary | Secondary |
| MRR/ARR | - | Primary | Primary |
| Growth rate | - | Primary | Primary |
| Burn rate / runway | Primary | Primary | Secondary |
| Unit economics | - | Emerging | Primary |
| Market share | - | - | Primary |

## Strategic Value Extraction

### Value Creation Levers

| Lever | Description | Timeline |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Customer introductions** | Connect portfolio company to corporate customers | Immediate |
| **Distribution access** | Leverage corporate channels | 3-6 months |
| **Technology collaboration** | Joint development, API integration | 6-12 months |
| **Talent exchange** | Secondments, mentoring, hiring pipeline | Ongoing |
| **Market intelligence** | Share market insights, customer feedback | Ongoing |
| **Operational support** | Legal, finance, HR shared services | As needed |
| **Co-selling** | Joint sales motions, bundle offers | 6-12 months |
| **Acquisition pipeline** | CVC as acquisition scouting | 2-5 years |

### Measuring Strategic Return

| Metric | Description | Measurement |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Strategic initiatives** | New initiatives inspired by portfolio | Count per year |
| **Technology adoption** | Portfolio tech used in corporate products | Integration count |
| **Market intelligence** | Insights gained from portfolio companies | Qualitative assessment |
| **Talent pipeline** | Hires from portfolio company network | Hires per year |
| **Revenue influenced** | Corporate revenue enabled by portfolio partnerships | Revenue attribution |
| **Optionality created** | Acquisition or partnership options generated | Options count |

## Innovation Investment Patterns

### Innovation Horizon Model

| Horizon | Focus | Risk | Return | Timeline | Vehicle |
|---------|-------|------|--------|----------|---------|
| **H1** | Core business extension | Low | Predictable | 0-2 years | Internal R&D, acqui-hire |
| **H2** | Adjacent opportunities | Medium | Moderate | 2-5 years | Partnerships, CVC (growth stage) |
| **H3** | Transformative bets | High | High potential | 5-10 years | CVC (early stage), JV, incubator |

### Investment Allocation by Horizon

| Approach | H1 | H2 | H3 |
|----------|-----|-----|-----|
| **Conservative** | 70% | 20% | 10% |
| **Balanced** | 60% | 25% | 15% |
| **Aggressive** | 50% | 25% | 25% |

### Build vs. Buy vs. Partner vs. Invest

| Factor | Build | Buy (M&A) | Partner | Invest (CVC) |
|--------|-------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Control** | Full | Full (post-integration) | Shared | Influence only |
| **Speed** | Slow | Medium | Medium | Fast (to learn) |
| **Cost** | Predictable | Large upfront | Moderate | Small upfront |
| **Risk** | Execution risk | Integration risk | Relationship risk | Portfolio risk |
| **Learning** | Deep but slow | Acquired knowledge | Shared learning | Market intelligence |
| **Reversibility** | Sunk cost | Difficult | Moderate | Exit possible |

**Decision rule**: Start with invest/partner for H3, graduate to build/buy as conviction increases.

## Startup Ecosystem Engagement

### Engagement Levels

| Level | Activities | Investment |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Observe** | Attend events, read reports, track startups | Minimal |
| **Engage** | Mentoring, hackathons, advisory roles | Time only |
| **Support** | Accelerator sponsorship, pilot programs | Moderate |
| **Invest** | Direct CVC investments | Significant |
| **Acquire** | M&A of validated startups | Major |

### Accelerator/Incubator Models

| Model | Control | Focus | Typical Cohort |
|-------|---------|-------|---------------|
| **Corporate accelerator** | Full | Strategic themes | 5-10 per cohort |
| **External accelerator sponsorship** | Low | Access to deal flow | Varies |
| **Innovation lab** | Full | Internal entrepreneurship | 2-5 projects |
| **Venture studio** | Full | Build new ventures from scratch | 2-3 per year |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Risk | Mitigation |
|-------------|------|-----------|
| **Tourist investing** | No strategic follow-through | Require strategic action plan per investment |
| **Conflict of interest** | Portfolio company vs. corporate product | Clear conflict policy, information barriers |
| **Slow decision-making** | Losing deals to faster VCs | Streamlined IC process (< 4 weeks) |
| **Strategic overreach** | Forcing portfolio companies into corporate agenda | Respect startup autonomy |
| **Vanity metrics** | Counting investments, not impact | Strategic + financial scorecards |
| **Orphaned investments** | No business unit sponsor | Require BU sponsor before investment |

## Term Sheet Essentials

### Key Terms for CVC

| Term | Standard | CVC-Specific Consideration |
|------|----------|---------------------------|
| **Valuation** | Market price | Strategic premium acceptable if thesis is strong |
| **Board seat** | Observer or board | Board if strategic value depends on it |
| **Information rights** | Standard VC | Ensure compliance with corporate info barriers |
| **Pro-rata rights** | Follow-on option | Important for maintaining strategic position |
| **Strategic rights** | Not standard | Right of first refusal, technology access, co-development |
| **Non-compete** | Rare at VC level | Avoid — damages founder relationship |
| **Acquisition rights** | Not standard | ROFR or preferential rights (negotiate carefully) |

### Co-Investment Strategy

| Strategy | When | Benefit |
|----------|------|---------|
| **Lead** | High conviction, strategic | Maximum influence, set terms |
| **Co-lead** | Strong conviction, share risk | Good influence, shared diligence |
| **Follow** | Learning stage, trust lead | Lower cost, leveraged diligence |
| **SPV/Syndicate** | Small check, relationship building | Access, minimal commitment |

## Common Pitfalls

- Startup valuations at early stages are highly subjective — present ranges, not point estimates
- CVC deal flow metrics must come from actual pipeline data
- Innovation portfolio theory requires multiple bets — single-bet recommendations need strong justification
