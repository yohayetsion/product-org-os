## Content Type Templates

### Blog Post Structure

**When to use**: Owned media content targeting search intent or thought leadership.

**Standard structure:**

```
1. Headline (H1) — primary keyword + benefit or curiosity gap
2. Introduction (100-150 words)
   - Hook: stat, question, or bold claim
   - Problem statement: why this matters to the reader
   - Thesis: what this post covers and what they'll get
3. Body (organized by H2 sections)
   - Lead with the most valuable insight, not context
   - One idea per section; H2 = topic, H3 = subtopic
   - Use data, examples, and quotes to support each point
4. Conclusion (100-150 words)
   - Summarize key takeaways (not a repeat of the body)
   - Practical next step or action prompt
5. CTA
   - One primary CTA aligned to funnel stage
   - Secondary CTA (newsletter, related content) optional
```

**Word count by goal:**

| Goal | Word Count | Rationale |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| SEO / search ranking | 1,500-2,500 | Sufficient depth for topical authority |
| Thought leadership | 800-1,500 | Prioritizes argument quality over length |
| Product update / announcement | 400-800 | Focused, timely |
| Pillar / cornerstone content | 2,500-5,000 | Comprehensive resource that earns links |

### Social Media Templates

**LinkedIn post structure (B2B):**

```
Line 1-2: Hook (the "stop the scroll" — visible before "see more")
[blank line]
Body: 3-7 short paragraphs, 1-2 sentences each
[blank line]
Takeaway or question to drive comments
[blank line]
Optional: 3-5 hashtags
```

**Twitter/X thread structure:**

```
Tweet 1: Bold claim or provocative question (the hook)
Tweet 2-N: One idea per tweet, building the argument
Last tweet: Summary + CTA (follow, link, or reply)
```

**Instagram caption structure:**

```
Line 1: Hook (visible without expanding)
Lines 2-5: Body — story, value, or context
Line 6: CTA (tag a friend, link in bio, save this)
Hashtags: 10-20, in first comment or after line break
```

### Email Newsletter Template

**When to use**: Owned-channel relationship building and content distribution.

```
Subject line: [curiosity gap or benefit, < 50 chars]
Preview text: [extends subject line, 40-90 chars]

Header/intro (2-3 sentences):
  - What's in this issue
  - Why it matters right now

Section 1: Featured content or insight
  - Headline
  - 2-3 paragraph summary
  - "Read more →" link

Section 2: Curated content (3-5 items)
  - Title + source
  - One-sentence description of why it matters

Section 3: Community / product update (optional)
  - 1-2 sentences max

Footer CTA:
  - Primary CTA aligned to business goal
  - Unsubscribe link (required by CAN-SPAM / GDPR)
```

### Landing Page Template

**When to use**: Conversion-focused pages for campaigns, product launches, lead gen.

```
1. Above the fold
   - Headline: Primary value proposition (what + for whom)
   - Subheadline: Supporting benefit or differentiation
   - Hero image or video (product, outcome, or social proof)
   - Primary CTA button (action-oriented label)

2. Problem / Situation (optional but high-converting)
   - 1-3 sentences acknowledging the reader's pain
   - Creates relevance and emotional connection

3. Solution / How It Works
   - 3-5 feature/benefit pairings
   - Use benefit-led language ("so you can..."), not feature lists

4. Social Proof
   - Customer quotes with name, title, company
   - Logos of recognizable customers or press
   - Metrics: "X companies trust us" / "Y% improvement"

5. Objection Handling
   - FAQ section addressing top 3-5 concerns
   - Risk reversal (free trial, money-back, no credit card)

6. Final CTA
   - Repeat primary CTA
   - Reinforce urgency or ease of getting started
```

### Case Study Template

**When to use**: Sales enablement, trust-building, demonstrating ROI.

```
Title: How [Customer] [Achieved Outcome] with [Product/Approach]

1. Customer context (2-3 sentences)
   - Industry, size, key role of protagonist

2. The challenge
   - Specific problem they faced (in their own words if possible)
   - Business impact of the problem (cost, risk, missed opportunity)

3. Why they chose [Product/Solution]
   - Key decision criteria
   - Why alternatives didn't work

4. Implementation
   - How they deployed or adopted the solution
   - Timeline and team involved

5. Results
   - 3 quantified outcomes (e.g., 40% reduction in X, 2x improvement in Y)
   - 1 qualitative outcome (quote or story)

6. What's next
   - How they're expanding or building on the success

Pull quote: One powerful sentence from the customer, displayed prominently.
```

### Whitepaper Template

**When to use**: Gated lead generation, thought leadership, ABM.

```
Title: [Specific Insight or Framework] — [Audience Benefit]

1. Executive summary (1 page)
   - The problem this paper addresses
   - Key findings (3-5 bullets)
   - Primary recommendation

2. Introduction (1-2 pages)
   - Industry context and why this topic matters now
   - Methodology (how insights were gathered)

3. The problem in depth (2-3 pages)
   - Data, research, or market evidence
   - Common approaches and why they fall short

4. Framework or model (2-3 pages)
   - Named framework or approach
   - Visual diagram where applicable
   - How it addresses the problem

5. Case evidence (1-2 pages)
   - 2-3 examples illustrating the framework in action

6. Recommendations (1-2 pages)
   - 3-5 actionable steps for the reader
   - Maturity model or diagnostic if applicable

7. Conclusion + next steps
   - Summary and call to action
```

### Content Pillars

**When to use**: Defining the 3-5 topic areas that will anchor all content production.

**Content pillar criteria:**
- Intersects audience pain points AND company expertise
- Broad enough for 10+ content pieces but focused enough to own
- Aligned to business goals (pipeline, retention, brand)
- Defensible — not just "everyone writes about this"

**Content pillar structure:**

```
Pillar: [Topic name]
Audience: [Which segment this serves]
Business goal: [Pipeline / Retention / Brand awareness]
Search intent: [Informational / Navigational / Commercial / Transactional]
Sub-topics (clusters):
  - [Sub-topic 1]
  - [Sub-topic 2]
  - [Sub-topic 3]
Key content format: [Blog / Video / Research / Tool]
```

**Common pillar frameworks by stage:**

| Funnel Stage | Pillar Focus | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Awareness | Industry problem or trend | "The State of [Topic]" |
| Consideration | Approach comparison | "How to Choose Between X and Y" |
| Decision | Proof and differentiation | Customer outcomes, ROI data |
| Retention | Advanced usage, community | "Expert guides", best practices |

## SEO Content Guidelines

### Keyword Research Process

**Step 1 — Seed keyword generation:**
- Start with customer language from sales calls, support tickets, reviews
- Map to product features, use cases, and problems solved
- Use competitor URLs as starting points for gap analysis

**Step 2 — Keyword evaluation criteria:**

| Criterion | Signal | Tool |
|-----------|--------|------|
| **Search volume** | Demand exists | Ahrefs, SEMrush, GSC |
| **Keyword difficulty** | Can we rank? | Ahrefs KD score |
| **Search intent** | Does it match our content? | SERP analysis |
| **Business relevance** | Does traffic convert? | Internal conversion data |
| **Trend** | Growing or declining? | Google Trends |

**Step 3 — Keyword prioritization matrix:**

| Priority | Volume | Difficulty | Business Relevance | Action |
|----------|--------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| High | Medium-High | Low-Medium | High | Create now |
| Medium | Low-Medium | Low | High | Create in next quarter |
| Low | High | High | Low | Deprioritize |
| Skip | Any | Very High | Low | Do not pursue |

---

### On-Page Optimization Checklist

**For every piece of SEO content:**

- [ ] Primary keyword in H1 (naturally, not forced)
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in meta title (< 60 chars)
- [ ] Compelling meta description with keyword (< 160 chars)
- [ ] Secondary keywords in H2/H3 headers
- [ ] Image alt text describes the image and includes keyword where natural
- [ ] Internal links to 2-5 relevant existing pages
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (opens in new tab)
- [ ] URL slug is short, descriptive, and includes primary keyword
- [ ] Schema markup for appropriate content type (Article, HowTo, FAQ)
- [ ] Content length matches or exceeds the median of top 5 SERP results
- [ ] No keyword stuffing — keyword density should feel natural (1-2%)

---

### Meta Description Framework

**Formula:**
```
[Action verb + what the page offers]. [Specific benefit or differentiator]. [CTA or next step].
```

**Examples:**

```
Bad:  "This page is about project management software features."
Good: "Compare 7 project management approaches and find the one that fits your team size. See pros, cons, and real switching costs."
```

**Rules:**
- 150-160 characters (Google truncates beyond this)
- Include primary keyword
- Match the intent of the page — don't oversell
- Write for the click, not the algorithm (Google often rewrites descriptions anyway)

---

### Internal Linking Strategy

**When to use**: Every time a new piece of content is published.

**Linking priority:**

| Link Type | Priority | Rationale |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Topic cluster → Pillar page | High | Passes authority to cornerstone content |
| New content → Related existing content | High | Distributes link equity, reduces orphaned pages |
| High-traffic page → Commercial pages | High | Converts organic visitors |
| Product pages → Supporting content | Medium | Helps buyers self-educate |

**Internal link checklist per post:**
- [ ] Link to the pillar page this cluster content supports
- [ ] Link to 2-4 related blog posts (same topic cluster)
- [ ] Include one contextual CTA linking to a product or landing page
- [ ] Update 2-3 existing posts to link back to this new content

## Headline Formulas

**When to use**: Writing any content headline — blog, email, social, ad.

| Formula | Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Number + Noun + Outcome** | "[N] [Things] that [Benefit]" | "7 Email Sequences That Doubled Our Pipeline" |
| **How To + Outcome** | "How to [Action] [Without/In/So You Can]" | "How to Reduce Churn Without Discounting" |
| **The [Adjective] Guide** | "The [Adj] Guide to [Topic]" | "The Practical Guide to B2B Content Strategy" |
| **Question** | "[Question your audience is asking]" | "Is Your Onboarding Killing Retention?" |
| **Contrast** | "[Common Belief] vs. [What's Actually True]" | "Content Volume vs. Content Depth: What Actually Drives Traffic" |
| **Myth Buster** | "Stop [Doing X]" / "Why [X] Is Wrong" | "Stop Writing Weekly Blogs and Do This Instead" |
| **Urgency** | "[Time-bound opportunity or threat]" | "The Window for SEO Content Advantage Is Closing" |
| **Social Proof** | "How [Company/Person] [Achieved Outcome]" | "How Intercom Grew to $150M ARR With Content" |
| **Secret / Insider** | "What [Experts] Know About [Topic]" | "What Top CMOs Know About Attribution That Others Don't" |
| **Before/After** | "[From X to Y]" / "[Before/After]" | "From 500 to 50,000 Monthly Visitors in 18 Months" |
| **Problem Agitate** | "[Pain] Is [Worse Than You Think]. Here's What To Do." | "Your Blog Traffic Is Lying to You. Here's How to Read It Right." |
| **Direct Benefit** | "[Achieve Outcome] With [Method or Tool]" | "Rank on Page 1 Without Paying for Backlinks" |

**Headline testing rules:**
- Write 10 headlines before choosing one
- Test A/B where possible (email subject lines, paid promotion)
- Front-load the value — first 3 words carry the most weight
- Keep under 70 characters for SEO; under 50 for social

## Content Distribution Matrix

**When to use**: Planning promotion immediately after content is produced. Content without distribution is wasted.

| Content Type | Owned (Email, Blog) | Earned (PR, Backlinks) | Paid (Ads, Sponsored) | Social (LinkedIn, Twitter) |
|-------------|--------------------|-----------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| **Blog post** | Newsletter, internal links | HARO, journalist outreach | LinkedIn Promoted Post, Google Ads | LinkedIn article share, Twitter thread |
| **Research / Report** | Email to full list | Press release, data journalism | LinkedIn Lead Gen ads | Data highlights as social posts |
| **Video** | Email embed | YouTube organic, press | YouTube Ads, pre-roll | LinkedIn native video |
| **Case study** | Sales sequence, onboarding | Customer social sharing | Retargeting ads | Customer quote as social post |
| **Whitepaper** | Gated email landing page | Analyst outreach | LinkedIn Lead Gen ads | Teaser statistics on social |

**Distribution checklist (post-publish):**
- [ ] Email to relevant subscriber segment within 24 hours

- [ ] Notify any sources, contributors, or partners mentioned
- [ ] Outreach to 3-5 relevant sites for backlink opportunity
- [ ] Add to active sales sequences if bottom-funnel content
- [ ] Submit URL to Google Search Console for indexing

## Content Performance Metrics

**When to use**: Selecting the right KPIs for each content type. Vanity metrics (raw pageviews) often obscure what actually matters.

| Content Type | Primary Metric | Secondary Metrics | Business Metric |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| **Blog / SEO** | Organic sessions | Keyword ranking position, bounce rate, time on page | Pipeline from organic search |
| **Email newsletter** | Click-through rate (CTR) | Open rate, unsubscribe rate, list growth rate | Revenue influenced per issue |
| **Social posts** | Engagement rate (engagements / impressions) | Follower growth, reach, shares | Profile visits, link clicks |
| **Landing page** | Conversion rate | Time on page, scroll depth, exit rate | Leads / MQLs generated |
| **Case study** | Sales cycle influence | Downloads, shares, time-on-page | Deals closed where case study was used |
| **Whitepaper** | Qualified downloads | Completion rate, form fill quality | MQLs from gated content |
| **Webinar** | Attendance rate (registered → attended) | Q&A engagement, replay views | Opportunities created post-event |
| **Video** | Watch-through rate | Plays, shares, comments | CTA click rate |

**Benchmarks (B2B SaaS, directional only):**

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|--------|------|---------|--------|
| Blog organic CTR | < 1% | 1-3% | > 4% |
| Email CTR | < 2% | 2-4% | > 5% |
| Landing page conversion | < 2% | 3-7% | > 10% |
| LinkedIn organic engagement | < 1% | 1-3% | > 4% |
| Webinar attendance rate | < 25% | 35-45% | > 55% |

**Limitations**: Benchmarks vary by industry, audience size, and channel maturity. Use your own historical data as the primary benchmark after 6+ months of operation.

## Repurposing Framework

**When to use**: Maximizing the value of content already produced. One original piece should generate 5-10 derivative assets.

**The Content Multiplier:**

```
Original Asset
    │
    ├── Long-form blog post (2,000 words)
    │       ├── Email newsletter (1 featured section)
    │       ├── LinkedIn article (adapted version)
    │       ├── Twitter/X thread (key points)
    │       ├── LinkedIn carousel (visual summary)
    │       ├── Short-form video script (60-90 second explainer)
    │       ├── Infographic (data or framework visualization)
    │       ├── Podcast talking points (discussion guide)
    │       └── Sales enablement one-pager (condensed for reps)
    │
    └── Research report (gated, 10+ pages)
            ├── Blog post (key finding summary)
            ├── Press release (top 3 stats)
            ├── Data story social posts (1 stat per post, series)
            ├── Webinar (deep dive on findings)
            └── Infographic (methodology + top findings)
```

**Repurposing decision matrix:**

| Original | Best Derivatives | Avoid |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Long-form blog | Thread, email, carousel | Full article repost (duplicate content) |
| Podcast episode | Quote cards, show notes blog, short clips | Transcript post (poor UX) |
| Webinar | Replay blog, clip highlights, Q&A post | Full unedited recording as primary asset |
| Customer quote | Social post, case study, email snippet | Using without permission |
| Research data | Stat posts, chart images, headline variations | Misrepresenting data out of context |

## Brand Voice Calibration Guide

**When to use**: Onboarding new writers, ensuring consistency across channels and contributors.

### Voice Dimensions

| Dimension | Options | Choose One per Brand |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| **Formality** | Formal ↔ Conversational ↔ Casual |  |
| **Tone** | Authoritative ↔ Collaborative ↔ Provocative ↔ Educational |  |
| **Perspective** | Expert advisor / Peer / Guide / Challenger |  |
| **Complexity** | Technical expert ↔ Accessible explainer |  |
| **Humor** | None ↔ Dry wit ↔ Playful |  |

### Voice Calibration Template

```
Brand: [Company name]
We are: [3 adjectives that describe the brand's voice]
We are not: [3 adjectives that would misrepresent the brand]

We write like: [Describe the "person" writing — role, personality]
We do not write like: [What to avoid]

Examples of on-brand copy:
  - "[Example sentence]"
  - "[Example sentence]"

Examples of off-brand copy:
  - "[Example sentence — and why it's wrong]"
  - "[Example sentence — and why it's wrong]"

Words we own (use frequently): [List 5-10 brand vocabulary terms]
Words we avoid: [List 5-10 terms that feel off-brand or overused by competitors]
```

### Common Voice Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Description | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| **Corporate speak** | "We are committed to delivering best-in-class solutions" | Write like a human explaining this to a colleague |
| **Inconsistent POV** | "We help teams" (company) vs. "I help teams" (individual) | Decide: brand voice or individual voice. Document it. |
| **Hedging everything** | "May potentially help some users possibly improve..." | Make claims with evidence; remove qualifiers |
| **Jargon without explanation** | Using industry terms assuming all readers know them | Define on first use, or replace with plain language |
| **Enthusiasm inflation** | Every feature is "game-changing", "revolutionary", "transformative" | Reserve superlatives for genuine differentiators |

## Content Audit Methodology

**When to use**: Quarterly review of existing content inventory, or before a major content strategy reset.

### Audit Process (5 Steps)

**Step 1 — Inventory**
Export all published URLs. Capture: title, publish date, word count, category/pillar, organic sessions (last 12 months), backlinks, conversion data.

**Step 2 — Classify each piece:**

| Classification | Criteria | Action |
|---------------|---------|--------|
| **Keep & Promote** | Top 20% by traffic and conversion | Promote actively, build internal links |
| **Update & Improve** | Ranking p. 2-5, outdated info, thin content | Refresh with new data, improve depth |
| **Consolidate** | Near-duplicate pieces on same topic | Merge into one comprehensive piece, 301 redirect others |
| **Remove & Redirect** | No traffic, no links, no strategic value | 301 redirect to closest relevant page |
| **Leave Alone** | Working but not a priority | Monitor quarterly |

**Step 3 — Gap analysis**
Map existing content against content pillars and funnel stages. Identify topics with no coverage and high search demand.

**Step 4 — Prioritize**
Rank by: (Potential traffic uplift × Business relevance) ÷ Production effort

**Step 5 — Execute**
Build a 90-day plan with specific actions for each classified piece.

**Audit cadence:**

| Audit Type | Frequency | Scope |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Full audit | Annual | Complete inventory |
| Performance review | Quarterly | Top 50 pieces by traffic |
| Quick check | Monthly | New content from last 30 days |
